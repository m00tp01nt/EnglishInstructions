How To Replace Your Laptop's Thermal Paste

These instructions are intended for laptop-owning tech-savvy do-it-yourself people whose laptops are overheating or slow/experiencing performance issues.

If you don't feel comfortable taking apart your laptop, call tech support.

What is thermal paste?

Thermal paste is a thermally conductive compound inside computers that helps distribute heat away from heat generating components. Over time it dries out, becoming less effective, and leading to overheating.

When thermal paste dries out, it should be replaced to keep your laptop running smoothly.

Step 1 - Is the problem your thermal paste?

Before doing anything, be sure the problem is dry thermal paste. Follow these instructions to check.

Listen to your laptop's fans

If your laptop's fans are always on, even when your laptop isn't doing anything, dry thermal paste could be the problem.

Keep in mind that if the environment the laptop is in is hot, the laptop will run hotter than normal. If this is the case, moving to a cooler or air-conditioned room may solve performance issues.

If the temperatures shown are below 50 degrees Celsius, temperature is probably not the thing slowing down your laptop.

If the temperatures are above 50 degrees Celsius, replacing the thermal paste could help solve your performance problem.

Step 2 - Tear down the laptop

Before we continue, make sure you have new thermal paste on hand! It would be the pits if you have your laptop all torn up only to realize you forgot to get good thermal paste.

Do some research to figure out what you need, but in general, you don't need anything too fancy. I got the cheapest I could find at my local Best Buy and it's worked fine.

These steps are general outlines for tearing down laptops, specifically those designed for gaming. Your laptop may have extra steps, or not need a particular step.

If you don't feel comfortable continuing while the laptop is still open, take a break. If after the break you still don't feel comfortable continuing, put the laptop back together, and contact professional tech support about your issue. Step 3 - Apply new thermal paste

For this section, it's important to get good airflow. I'm going to open the window I'm working by. If you're in a smaller room, just using a fan may not be enough, and you could have to move to a different room. Safety is key.

I'm also going to put on some gloves. Modern thermal paste isn't dangerous, but it may be difficult to clean off your hands.

When you're ready, continue to the next step.

Show All Steps

Wet your microfiber cloth with some isopropyl alcohol. I'm using a small glass cup so I can control how much I'm using easily.

Gently begin rubbing the CPU and GPU with the alcohol. This is going to loosen the dried thermal paste, and it will be transferred onto the microfiber cloth. After rubbing for a bit, switch to a new part of the microfiber cloth, re-wet it, and continue. Repeat until the surface of the CPU and GPU are reflective.

This is what we're going for. The GPU and CPU are reflective.

Remove the dry thermal paste from the cooling system.

Grab your thermal paste. Most of them are in a small syringe.

Remove the cap from the tube.

Position the tube above the GPU, and apply a pea-sized drop.

Since my CPU is not a square, I'm also going to trace a line down the middle to ensure the entire surface will be covered in thermal paste when the cooling system is reapplied.

Step 5 - Testing

Now that the laptop is reassembled with fresh thermal paste, turn it on and start MSI Afterburner.

Open the Hardware Monitor, and check if the temperature of your components has gone down.

If they have, but you're still having performance issues, you can take dry thermal paste off your list of possibilities.
